Program overview

This is a youth production of The Sound of Music directed by Nell Henry (age 14) and Coraline Hewitt (age 13). A chaparone will be present at all times. The cast will be 25–35 students, primarily ages 6–12, Rehearsals run three days a week for eight weeks and culminate in three performances at the Juanita High School Performing Arts Center.

Rehearsals: Local school multipurpose room or church hall in Kirkland (TBD)

Performances: Juanita High School Performing Arts Center

Who can participate?

  • Ages 6–12 (no prior experience required)
  • Students who enjoy singing, acting, or trying something new
  • Older teens may be invited for select adult roles or tech crew (TBD)
  • Auditions are required.
This is a medium-level production: real sets, costumes, and props—but with a focus on learning, teamwork, and fun rather than perfection.

What auditions look like

Auditions are designed to be low-stress and supportive. We want to see each child’s personality, willingness to try, and comfort on stage—not perfection.

What to prepare

  • 30–60 second monologue (can be from a play, book, or memorized story)
  • 30–60 second song (musical theatre, folk song, or any age-appropriate piece)

During the audition

  • Students audition in groups of 3–6
  • Each group has about 30 minutes
  • Simple movement or acting games may be included

After auditions

  • Cast list emailed to families
  • Rehearsal schedule and materials shared
  • All registered students will be considered for a role or ensemble

Production details

Sets & costumes

  • Medium-level sets built by the production team
  • Thrifted and simply sewn costumes for all main groups
  • Organized costume racks and quick-change areas backstage

Props & tech

  • Lightweight, safe props (lantern, guitar, whistle, suitcases, etc.)
  • Student tech crew for lights, sound, props, and costumes
  • Clearly labeled prop tables and backstage organization

Supervision & staffing

  • At least one adult supervisor at all rehearsals
  • Two adults backstage during performances
  • Student stage manager and assistant director roles

Safety, forms, and legal basics

This program follows standard youth theatre practices to keep students safe and to respect the rights of the creators of The Sound of Music.

Licensing & rights

  • Official performance license obtained from the rights holder.
  • Approved script and score are used.
  • No major changes to plot or lyrics without permission.
  • No recording or livestreaming of full performances unless allowed by the license.

Required forms for each student

  • Registration form – basic contact information.
  • Medical/emergency form – allergies and emergency instructions.
  • Media/photo release – permission to use photos for marketing and cast sharing.

These can be completed online and will be sent to you after you submit the registration form below.

Safety & venue

  • Sign-in/sign-out system so children leave only with an approved adult.
  • First aid kit on site; emergency contacts kept on file.
  • All Juanita PAC safety and fire rules are followed.
  • Chaparone on site at all times.
  • Event insurance may be carried if required by the school district.
